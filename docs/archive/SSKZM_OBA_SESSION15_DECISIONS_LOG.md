## PART 1 — CONSTITUTIONAL AND STRUCTURAL DECISIONS

### EC Handover
- EC handover takes place at the AGM after the formal announcement of results
- Incoming EC takes charge on AGM day itself at the moment of formal announcement
- RO files election record and surrenders credentials to the incoming EC on AGM day after formal announcement
- The 7-day filing window is removed — filing happens on AGM day

### Election Period
- Election period definition retained as "date of filing of the election record with the General Secretary"
- This automatically covers the contingency where the election extends beyond AGM day
- Section 2.6 retitled: **"Election Period — Authority and Continuity"**

### Contingency — Voting Cannot Close by AGM Day
Three insertion points agreed and finalised:

**Section 2.6 — final approved text to add:**
*"Where the voting window cannot be closed by AGM day due to mandatory posts not being nominated, technical failure or other contingency, the election period shall extend until the voting window is closed and results are declared. The Returning Officer's authority shall continue for the duration of the extended period. The outgoing Executive Committee shall continue in a caretaker capacity with powers as specified by the General Body until the election period concludes and the incoming Executive Committee takes charge."*

**Section 7.6 — full approved rewrite:**
*"In the event of a technical failure of the voting platform during the voting window, the Returning Officer shall change the election status to paused through the admin panel. This transition is recorded automatically in the AdminLog. The Returning Officer shall cause a notification to be sent to all members that the voting window is temporarily suspended, stating that a technical review is underway.*

*The RO, with the assistance of the TEM where appointed, shall review the AdminLog and the Google account access log to determine the nature and extent of the failure. The following staged response shall apply:*

*Stage 1 — Minor interruption: Where the failure is a technical malfunction with no evidence of data compromise, the RO may restore the system and reopen the voting window after satisfying themselves that the system is functioning correctly. The voting window may be extended by the duration of the suspension to compensate voters who were unable to cast their votes during the interruption.*

*Stage 2 — Extended outage: Where the system cannot be restored promptly, the RO shall refer the matter to the Executive Committee. The Executive Committee shall decide the course of action, which may include continuing efforts to restore the system and extending the voting window beyond AGM day under the provisions of Section 2.6. Efforts to restore the system and conclude the election shall continue for as long as necessary under the extended election period provisions of Section 2.6.*

*Stage 3 — Data integrity compromise: Where there is evidence of tampering with the Votes or VotedLog tabs, the election cannot proceed on the current platform until rectified. The RO shall notify all members and the Executive Committee immediately. The matter shall be placed before the General Body at the AGM, which shall decide the course of action. The options available to the General Body include conducting fresh electronic elections with accepted nominations and the scrutinised candidate list preserved, or such other course as the General Body deems fit. The provisions of Section 2.6 regarding continuation of the outgoing Executive Committee in a caretaker capacity shall apply pending conclusion of the election.*

*All actions taken during and after a system failure shall be recorded in detail in the election record."*

**Section 9.6 — final approved rewrite:**
*"The Returning Officer shall formally announce the election results at the Annual General Body Meeting. Where results have been declared prior to the AGM under Section 8.5, the announcement at the AGM shall be a formal confirmation of those results. The incoming Executive Committee shall take charge at the AGM upon the formal announcement. Where the election has not been concluded by AGM day, the matter shall be placed before the General Body and the provisions of Section 2.6 shall apply."*

**Bylaw — new Clause (m):**
*"Where the election cannot be concluded by the date of the Annual General Body Meeting, the outgoing Executive Committee shall continue in a caretaker capacity with powers as determined by the General Body at the Annual General Body Meeting, until the incoming Executive Committee takes charge upon conclusion of the election."*

## PART 5 — SCRUTINEERS DECISIONS

### Appointment
- Appointed by EC, not RO
- Odd number, minimum two (bylaw), minimum two and maximum four (SOP)
- Section 2A.7 rewritten — appointing authority changed from RO to EC

### Digital Acceptance Flow (🔴 Code build required)
1. EC decides, Gen Sec sends appointment email to each Scrutineer (copy to RO)
2. RO enters Scrutineer in system (name, roll, role)
3. System sends acceptance link to Scrutineer
4. Scrutineer accepts — constitutes Declaration of Impartiality
5. System notifies Gen Sec and RO
6. Gen Sec confirms receipt
7. System activates Scrutineer credentials, notifies both
8. Non-acceptance within specified period → system alerts Gen Sec and RO for replacement
9. Manual document attachment option available at every stage

### Declaration of Impartiality
- Appendix G split into two forms:
  - **G1 — TEM Declaration** (witnessed by RO)
  - **G2 — Scrutineer Declaration** (witnessed by General Secretary)
- Familial clause removed from both forms
- No Declaration of Impartiality required for Appeals Panel or RO/Dy RO

### AdminLog Access
- Scrutineers to have **full AdminLog read-only access from the beginning of the election period**
- This is a new tab in the Scrutineer tab bar

### Bylaw Clause (d) — Final Approved Text
*"The Executive Committee shall appoint not less than two Scrutineers to witness and verify the election process. Scrutineers shall be Life Members of the Association who are not contesting any post and are not members of the current Executive Committee. The Returning Officer may accredit Observers for the election, who may include representatives nominated by contesting candidates. Observers shall have access to aggregate participation data only and shall have no access to individual voter information or vote data. The duties of Scrutineers and Observers and the procedure for their appointment shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

## PART 6 — APPEALS PANEL DECISIONS

### Constitution
- Constituted by EC from RO panel in order of seniority
- Odd number, minimum three
- Most senior member = Chair
- Constituted simultaneously with Scrutineers
- No Declaration of Impartiality required

### Jurisdiction
- Nomination rejections — rejected candidates may appeal
- Any decision or sanction under election rules — any directly affected candidate or member may appeal
- Code of Conduct: RO = first instance for all complaints; Appeals Panel = appeals against RO decisions/sanctions
- Appeals Panel decision **final for current election**
- General Body = post-election governance grievances only

### Timeline
- New Section 1.2 entry: Appeals Panel constitution timeline
- New Appendix A row at V-36: EC constitutes Appeals Panel

### Appendix F.2 — New Item
- "Record of any Appeals Panel proceedings and decisions — filed: Yes / No / Not applicable"

### Section 8.6 — New Bullet
- "Record of any Appeals Panel proceedings and decisions, including appeals received, decisions made, and reasons"

### Bylaw Clause (h) — Final Approved Text
*"The Executive Committee shall constitute an Appeals Panel drawn from the panel published for RO selection. The Appeals Panel shall comprise an odd number of members, with a minimum of three. The Appeals Panel shall have no role unless an appeal is filed. A candidate whose nomination is rejected may appeal to the Appeals Panel. Any candidate or member directly affected by a decision or sanction under these rules may appeal to the Appeals Panel. The decision of the Appeals Panel shall be final for the purposes of the current election. Nothing in this clause shall prejudice the right of any member to raise a governance grievance with the General Body. The procedure for constitution, appeals, and decisions shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

## PART 7 — NOMINATIONS DECISIONS

### One-Post-Per-Person Rule
- Phase 1: second nomination blocked if active nomination exists — must withdraw first
- Phase 2: consent for second post blocked if consent already given — must withdraw first
- System-enforced + SOP

### Candidature Withdrawal
- Section 4.5 rewritten as unified withdrawal section covering all stages
- Withdrawal possible at any stage up to candidature withdrawal deadline
- **Candidature withdrawal deadline:** end of day immediately following candidate list publication (D+1 1700hrs if published D+0 1700hrs)
- **Voting window opens:** not earlier than 72 hours after candidate list publication (D+3 1700hrs)
- System must lock withdrawals after deadline
- Section 5.6 (Withdrawal During Scrutiny) — deleted, cross-reference to Section 4.5 inserted
- Section 5.9 — add withdrawal deadline notification requirement
- Section 7.8 — note withdrawal window between candidates_published and deadline

### Nomination Form
- Appendix B — retained as is (declaration "not contesting any other post" retained)

### Scrutiny Checklist
- Appendix C — new item: "Candidate has no active accepted nomination for another post in this election — confirmed by system"

### Mandatory Posts Threshold
- President, VP1 (Senior Vice President), General Secretary, Treasurer = mandatory posts
- Standard extensions: two × 7 days maximum
- After standard extensions exhausted for mandatory posts: continuous further extensions in 2–7 day increments, no cap, until filled or AGM day
- "Mandatory post unfilled" sufficient as standing reason for continuous extensions — no further written justification required
- Each extension communicated to all members immediately
- If still unfilled at AGM: General Body makes live floor nomination attempt
- If still unfilled after floor attempt: General Body votes on (a) proceed — vacant, co-option for that post OR (b) halt entire election — fresh elections, nominations data and scrutinised candidate list preserved, period flexible as decided by GB
- System hard-block on `candidates_published → active` for mandatory posts with no nomination
- GB override pathway: RO records General Body resolution in system — lifts hard-block
- Section 4.10 cross-reference error fixed: "section 4.8" should be "section 4.9"

### Extension Flexibility
- Extensions in 2–7 day increments (not compulsorily 7 days)
- Maximum single extension 7 days
- Minimum single extension 2 days

### Stage Compression
- Voting window: minimum 7 days, compressible to floor of 5 days
- Scrutiny period: minimum 7 days, compressible to floor of 3 days
- Conditions: only where earlier stage has overrun and compression necessary to complete before AGM day
- Must be communicated to all members immediately with reasons
- Recorded in AdminLog
- No compression once voting window has opened
- No compression of constitutional objection windows

### Section 4.1 — President Rule Reference
- Add: "(as per bylaw amendment dated 20 July 2024)" after President heading

### Section 4.10 — Minimum Posts
- Mandatory posts (P/VP1/GS/T) explicitly listed and stated as non-removable from minimum posts list
- RO may add further posts
- For four mandatory posts: GB-only authorisation to proceed without — no RO override

### Uncontested Posts — Section 5.7
- Single nomination for any post (including mandatory) = uncontested, declared elected without vote
- No nomination for mandatory post after all efforts = election halts, matter to General Body
- No nomination for non-mandatory post = vacant, co-option

### Bylaw Clause (i) — Final Approved Text
*"Where only one valid nomination is received for any post, the candidate shall be declared elected without proceeding to a vote. Where no valid nomination is received for a post other than the posts of President, Vice President (Senior), General Secretary, and Treasurer, the post shall remain vacant and shall be filled by co-option by the incoming Executive Committee in accordance with the Bylaws. Where no valid nomination is received for any of the posts of President, Vice President (Senior), General Secretary, or Treasurer after all efforts have been exhausted, the election shall not proceed and the matter shall be placed before the General Body to decide the course of action. The procedure shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

## PART 10 — VOTING DECISIONS

### Mode of Voting — Section 7.1
- Decoupled from AGM mode
- Electronic preferred for wider participation
- Physical requires reasons recorded, communicated to members, placed before next AGM
- Physical = preferably secret ballot
- Mode of voting = only the manner in which votes are cast and counted; full election procedure stands regardless
- Historical practice clarification required — avoid impression that all nominations etc happen on AGM day

### Results Declaration
- Electronic elections: not less than 10 days before AGM
- Physical elections: on AGM day
- Bylaw: "declared at earliest practicable time after verification" — principle-based
- Voting window close date at RO's discretion provided sufficient time for post-poll steps
- Stipulated voting window: 7 days minimum (compressible to 5 days per stage compression rule)

### Section 7.7 — Closing the Poll
- Remove implicit AGM-day tie
- Voting window close date set by RO

### Appendix A — Calendar Changes
- V-36 row: "Nomination call issued not later than V-36"
- New row: Candidature withdrawal deadline (D+1 from candidate list publication)
- New row: Results declared (not less than V-10 for electronic elections)
- V-0 row: Update — voting window close may be before AGM day; AGM = formal announcement only
- New row at V-36: EC constitutes Appeals Panel

### Appendix J Item D3 — Rewrite
- New D3: "Mandatory posts (President, VP1, General Secretary, Treasurer) all have accepted nominations. If not, voting window cannot open. Where General Body has authorised proceeding without a mandatory post, RO has recorded the General Body resolution in the system."

### Bylaw Clause (j) — Final Approved Text
*"Voting shall be conducted in such mode as decided by the Executive Committee, preferably by electronic means to ensure wider participation. The mode of voting shall be independent of the mode of conduct of the Annual General Body Meeting. Where physical voting is used, the reasons shall be recorded and communicated to all members and placed before the next Annual General Body Meeting. Where physical voting is used it shall preferably be by secret ballot. The mode of voting refers only to the manner in which votes are cast and counted. The full procedure of elections — including the selection of the Returning Officer, nominations, scrutiny, and publication of the candidate list — shall be conducted as specified in this clause and in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time, regardless of the mode of voting. Each Life Member on the certified voter roll shall have one vote per post for which they are eligible. The secrecy and integrity of the ballot shall be preserved in all modes. The procedure for voting, including authentication, voting window, and closing of poll, shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

## PART 14 — BYLAW APPENDIX D — REMAINING APPROVED CLAUSE TEXTS

### Clause (f) — Nominations
*"The Returning Officer shall issue a notification calling for nominations to all posts of the Executive Committee. Each nomination shall require the confirmation of a proposer and a seconder, both of whom shall be Life Members of the Association. A candidate may not be their own proposer or seconder. Nominations shall be submitted through such means as designated by the Returning Officer, which shall include provision for online submission. The procedure for nomination, including timelines, forms, and confirmation requirements, shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

### Clause (g) — Scrutiny
*"The Returning Officer shall scrutinise each nomination received and verify the eligibility of the candidate for the post sought. The Returning Officer may seek clarification from any candidate or from the Executive Committee on matters of institutional record. The Returning Officer shall communicate the acceptance or rejection of each nomination to the candidate concerned, with written reasons in the case of rejection. Upon completion of scrutiny, the Returning Officer shall publish the final list of candidates for each post. The procedure for scrutiny, including timelines, query procedure, withdrawal, and publication, shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

### Clause (l) — Candidate Eligibility
*"Candidate eligibility for each post shall be as specified in sub-sections (iv), (v), and (vi) of this Article, which shall apply equally regardless of the mode of voting. The detailed eligibility matrix and scrutiny procedure shall be as specified in the Operating Procedures laid down from time to time."*

### Clause (n) — Operating Procedures
*"The Executive Committee shall frame Operating Procedures for the conduct of elections consistent with this clause. Such Operating Procedures shall be placed before the Annual General Body Meeting for information. The Executive Committee may amend the Operating Procedures from time to time consistent with the Bylaws."*
